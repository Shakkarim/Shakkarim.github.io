Understanding severe weather and environmental extremes across scales

I am a Postdoctoral Research Associate at Hampton University’s Severe Weather Research Center. My work integrates Doppler radar retrievals, large-eddy simulations, remote sensing, and numerical weather prediction to investigate severe convection, turbulence, wildfire environments, urban-weather interactions, and tropical cyclone processes.

Summary

Atmospheric scientist with more than eight years of experience in multiscale numerical modeling, turbulence-resolving simulations, model validation, radar and satellite remote sensing, and high-performance computing. My recent work emphasizes radar-based 3D wind retrieval, severe weather forecasting, urban impacts on extreme weather, and process-based atmospheric dynamics.

Research Focus

My research spans Doppler radar wind retrieval, large-eddy simulation of severe weather, satellite-assimilated forecasting, wildfire meteorology, mountain meteorology, and tropical cyclone interaction with complex urban and terrain environments.
