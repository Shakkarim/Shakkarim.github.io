Atmospheric Scientist

Welcome. I am an atmospheric scientist working on radar meteorology, severe weather dynamics, large-eddy simulation, numerical weather prediction, and remote sensing applications. My research integrates observations, theory, and high-resolution modeling to better understand multiscale atmospheric processes and environmental extremes.

My current work at Hampton University focuses on Doppler radar wind retrieval, satellite-assimilated severe weather forecasting, turbulence in convection, and urban impacts on post-landfall tropical cyclone evolution.

Research Focus
  • Doppler radar wind retrieval and radar simulator development
  • Large-eddy simulation of severe convection and turbulence
  • Satellite-assimilated severe weather forecasting
  • Urban impacts on tropical cyclone dissipation
  • Wildfire meteorology and mountain weather dynamics
Methods and Tools

To investigate these problems, I use multi-Doppler radar datasets, satellite remote sensing, ensemble and high-resolution numerical models including WRF-ARW, WRF-LES, MPAS-A, HYSPLIT, and CM1, along with Python, MATLAB, Fortran, and high-performance computing workflows.

Latest News
  • Developing a ground-based Doppler radar algorithm to retrieve 3D winds from multi-radar platforms.
  • Maintaining a satellite-assimilated forecasting system for severe weather applications at SWRC.
  • Advancing research on tropical cyclone interaction with urban environments.
